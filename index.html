GroverGaming

As a Junior Game Developer, I develop engaging casino-styled electronic gambling games. I program unique and exciting ways to showcase the players' winnings and crafted attractive and interactive gameplay mechanics. While keeping the game's performance optimized, I collaborate closely with animators and artists to enhance our games with vibrant visual effects and worked with sound designers to integrate immersive audio. We make sure our games are captivating and alluring while also making sure our games hits all requirements including legal requirements. I enjoy tackling challenges from algorithmic solving to programming intriguing but difficult gameplay mechanics. I often find myself brainstorming with peers to develop new ways to solve certain distribution problems and more efficient game functionality. Since anybody developer needs to be able to iterate on any game, we make sure that our code are readable, concise, and to our coding standards. Working at GroverGaming also gave me first hand experience in working in a studio, allowing me to refine my communication, teamwork, and leadership skills. Overall, I am extremely glad to have my foot in the door to become a strong game developer.

ChangelingVR

A VR narrative mystery. This is a work in progress game by RIT. For my first internship, I worked as the sole sound design intern working on music, sound effects, and audio tunning for already established content and new ones designed this year. Throughout the internship, I have recorded and implemented Foley, composed a brand new background track for the mother's level, and synthesized sound effects that are not naturally found in real life. With the help of Audacity, Reaper, and Ableton Live, I was able to record, edit, and produce quality sound assets that allowed a player to truly be immersed in the game. In summer of 2023, I was invited back as a level designer to development the final level of the game. With a team of 11, we developed multiple prototypes of game mechanics and level layouts, then conducted many playtests of those prototypes to narrow down and come up with a final product. I worked on a mechanic that would launch objects in the air like a catapult with the touch of a finger. I also programmed walls to react to the launched objects, with its final stage being shattered to pieces and floating away. From there, I dervived of an idea to use this mechanic that would fit into the lore of the game and designed a level layout that would utilize this mechanic. Overall, I am pleased to not only be able to contribute to immersing the player with audio but also in developing the final climatic level from scratch. Amateur Rhythm Game

A casual rhythm game that had the idea of mobile gaming in mind. This project was made using Unity 2019 and assets created by team members. In the span of 10 weeks, my team and I built a rythm game that requires players to swipe the notes at the right time and with the correct direction. For this project, I was the main level designer and sound designer. Using Ableton, I composed three seperate minute long tracks to be used for each level. Then using Wwise, I implemented the song as well as the timing for level. I also constructed the arrays that determines where the note will appear and what direction does the player have to hit it in. Amatuer Rhythm Game was supposedly on mobile phones however, due to the time frame and other difficulties, it was out of scope and a desktop only version was made. However, we did keep the idea of having the game be a causal mobile game in mind. This is why you can see that each song is at most a minute and a half long and each level has its own local leaderboard and scoring. This gives the idea to players that they can pick up the game at anytime and play through a song quickly. There is even a pause function which allows the player to stop playing at moments noticed. DogFight

A short film animation made by Tim Caravan. In the span of 6 weeks, I composed a score for the film's background music using Reaper. This was my second time working with this amazing artist and together we developed a film that was featured in RIT's theatre. The short is about a captain's fleet struggle in space. Working with the artist, I composed the score to match the actions of the film. From the battle sequence having multiple strings and brass fighting for the spotlight to a soft and solo string playing when the captain has met his match, many of the actions in the film work correspond to what the score is doing. This project was a lot of back and forth with the artist with a lot of editing being done to the film and therefore the score as well. However, working on this project really helps me understand the process that composers do whenever they write a score for a film or even a game cinematic. Hopefully, I will be able to compose and work with more artists and eventually score video game cinematics professionally.
